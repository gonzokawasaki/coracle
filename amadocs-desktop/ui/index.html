Infrastructure Engineering
Annual Infrastructure
Review 2025
Platform Reliability & Capacity Planning
Rev. 2.1 · 24 pages · Confidential
1. Executive Summary

Over the 2025 fiscal year the platform sustained 99.98% availability across all primary regions, a 0.04 point improvement over the prior year. Total compute footprint grew 31% while unit cost per request fell 12%, driven largely by the migration of stateless services to the new autoscaling tier.

This document reviews capacity headroom by region, summarises the year's incident trends, and presents the proposed investment plan for the coming year.

2. Capacity & Utilisation

Aggregate CPU utilisation averaged 58% at peak, leaving comfortable headroom in every region except eu-west-2, which crossed the 80% threshold during three separate demand spikes in Q3.

3. Reliability & Incidents

Four Sev-1 incidents were recorded over the year, compared to seven in 2024. Mean time to recovery improved from 41 minutes to 23 minutes, attributable to the new runbook automation deployed in Q1. No incidents resulted in customer data loss.
